Melissa Houser

Founder and counselor, Melissa Houser began Sparkle Counseling in 2011, after five years of counseling at-risk individuals suffering from adjustment difficulties, severe traumas, attachment issues, and dual diagnoses. Although her experience equips her to counsel an array of issues of varying severity, her heart’s special passion is working with teens, young adult women, and children.

Training  and Education

Licensed in the State of Colorado as a Professional Counselor and certified as an Addictions Counselor Level III, she also holds various national certifications, and has recently completed training that will lead towards registration as a Registered Play Therapist. Currently completing her PhD in Counseling Psychology at the University of Denver, she holds a master’s degree in counseling from Colorado Christian University and a Bachelor’s of Music in Music Therapy from Appalachian State University.

Philosophy  and Approach

Melissa integrates experiential modalities such as sand trays, art, play, and music with traditional talk-therapy approaches of Cognitive-Behavioral therapy, Motivational Interviewing, and insight-oriented approaches.

Her goal is to weave these approaches together to work with an individual’s strengths, empowering them with a new self-story. She says, “I believe everyone has strengths that allow success, but sometimes they are forgotten due to trauma or abuse. A fresh perspective can put the whole story together, provide that reminder, and allow an individual to emerge-spark of life reignited.”
